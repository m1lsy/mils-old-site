Personal Leadership
Regarding personal leadership, I sometimes tend to lack self motivation, however this semester I have focused on having the ability to identify and seize opportunities. I did this through asking others for feedback and learning from others. Whether this be asking for various peoples opinions on something I am working on or looking at what others are working on as examples or inspirations for my own work. By remaining open to feedback, I am recognizing and addressing my own learning needs, and engaging in reflective practices to enhance personal growth. Reflection: One problem that I have encountered within this learning outcome is that I tend to not ask for feedback as often as I should, despite recognizing its importance. This is due to me adjusting to this new and very different system of learning where feedback is not just received and that I have to seek feedback and ask for it in order to get it. I also did not understand how important recording the feedback I received was, and that I should not just listen to the feedback but that I should write it down for future reflection. Unfortunately, I only came to this realisation at the end of the semester and thus I feel that I am lacking in evidence of this learning outcome. In my future semesters, I hope to make more use of Feedpulse and I aim to engage more with my teachers. Despite this problem, I still received very helpful feedback through out the semester from both my teachers and fellow students which was a great help to my work.
Investigative Problem Solving
In the realm of investigative problem-solving, my approach has been marked by possessing a set of skills and a certain attitude related to addressing problems in a systematic and inquisitive manner. To demonstate these aspects, I recognize the importance of questioning throughout the solution process, by not just accepting information given to me but probing deeper to gain a comprehensive understanding of the information I am taking in. This involves using different approaches to find a solution based on the nature of the problem. These various approaches include pragmatic problem-solving, critical thinking and the use of sources. Pragmatic problem solving involves being practical and realistic and is about finding solutions that are feasible and effective in the given context. Critical thinking means I need to assess information critically, evaluate its reliability, and make informed decisions. This involves analysing the evidence, identifying assumptions, and considering alternative perspectives before coming to a conclusion. Lastly, through using sources and researching information, I am able to find credible and reliable information to support problem-solving efforts. These approaches help me get a holistic understanding of the problem by considering different aspects and factors and going beyond the surface to understand the interconnected elements of a situation.
